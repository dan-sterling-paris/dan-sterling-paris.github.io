The Story

In a world of identical sausage dogs, one dachshund refuses to fit the mould.

Based on Rob Biddulph’s bestselling book, Odd Dog Out is a foot-stomping, tail-wagging musical celebration of individuality. It follows the journey of one busy dog who leaves her town to find her place in the world, only to discover that maybe standing out is the best way to fit in.

Bringing Doggywood to Life

A multi-layered theatrical experience combining pop music, puppetry, and live performance.

The Actor-Musician Orchestra

Instruments aren't hidden in a pit—they are an extension of the characters. The cast drives the action and the music simultaneously, creating a seamless, high-energy performance style.

A Pop-Fuelled Score

Written by the critically acclaimed Vikki Stone, the score blends sharp, witty lyricism for adults with an eclectic, vibrant pop sound that keeps children engaged from start to finish.

Puppetry & Visuals

The bustling world of Doggywood is realized through imaginative puppetry and a set design that faithfully recreates Rob Biddulph's signature colorful style.

Creative Biographies

Rob Biddulph

Author & Illustrator

Rob is a bestselling and multi-award-winning author/illustrator. He is the genius behind the viral sensation #DrawWithRob, a series of draw-along videos that garnered millions of views during lockdown.

His first picture book, Blown Away, won the Waterstones Children's Book Prize in 2015. His subsequent titles, including GRRRRR! and Odd Dog Out, have cemented his status as a titan of children's literature. Before becoming a full-time author, Rob was the art director for the Observer Magazine, NME, and Just Seventeen.

Odd Dog Out was published by HarperCollins in 2016 and celebrates the beauty of fitting out.

Vikki Stone

Book, Music & Lyrics

Vikki Stone is an award-winning writer, comedian, musician, and composer. She famously co-adapted the smash-hit TV show Hey Duggee for the stage, which won Best Family Show at the 2023 Olivier Awards.

Described by The Scotsman as "the bastard love child of Victoria Wood and Tim Minchin," Vikki is also the dramaturg on the new musical Burlesque. She is an Associate of The Royal Academy of Music (ARAM) and has appeared on numerous TV shows including Richard Osman’s House of Games and Romeo & Duet (ITV).

Tom Hardy

Voice Over

One of the most acclaimed actors of his generation, Tom Hardy lends his distinctive voice to the production, serving as the narrator and guide through the world of Doggywood.

Known for his roles in The Dark Knight Rises, Mad Max: Fury Road, and Peaky Blinders, Tom is also a celebrated reader of CBeebies Bedtime Stories, making him a familiar and beloved voice to children and parents alike.

Dan Blumenau

Producer

Founder of Sterling Paris Limited, Dan brings over 20 years of industry expertise in production and general management. He currently serves as a Commercial Consultant for Mamma Mia! The Party.

Previously, Dan served as a Financial Analyst for Disneyland Paris, managing finances for the resort's seven hotels and entertainment quarters. His unique blend of creative production experience and high-level corporate finance acumen ensures the commercial viability of the project.

Harry Blumenau

Producer

Harry is a prominent casting director for theatre, TV, and film. He won the 2025 WhatsOnStage Best Casting Direction Award for Why Am I So Single?.

His extensive credits include Beetlejuice The Musical (Prince Edward Theatre), Kinky Boots (London Coliseum/Tour), Death Note: The Musical (London Palladium), and Derren Brown: Showman (Channel 4). He brings a wealth of industry connections and artistic insight to the production team.

Repayment Structure

  • Priority Repayment: Investors will be repaid 100% of their initial capital investment from the first Net Receipts of the production.
  • Profit Share: Once 100% capitalisation is returned, investors will receive a pro-rata share of the production's profits (typically 50% or 60% distributed to the Investor Pool).

Theatre Tax Relief (TTR)

The production benefits from the UK Government's Theatre Tax Relief scheme, which significantly de-risks the investment by offering a cash rebate on qualifying production expenditure.
